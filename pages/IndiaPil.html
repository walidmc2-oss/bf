Sacred India Pilgrimages: Journey to the Heart of Devotion

Experience the Soul of India with Bhaktifield

Twice a year, Bhaktifield offers the sacred opportunity to embark on deeply transformative pilgrimages (Yatras) to the holy lands of India. These journeys are more than just travel; they are an immersion into the vibrant spiritual culture, ancient wisdom, and profound devotion that India offers. Join us as we explore sacred sites, connect with spiritual communities, and deepen our understanding of Bhakti Yoga.

Our pilgrimages are typically scheduled around March and October, aligning with some of the most auspicious Vaishnava festivals, allowing for a truly unique and spiritually charged experience.

The March Pilgrimage: Celebrating Gaura Purnima

Our pilgrimage around March is centered on celebrating Gaura Purnima, the divine appearance day of Lord Sri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. Lord Chaitanya is revered as the golden avatar who appeared in the 15th century to inaugurate the Yuga Dharma for this age ‚Äì Harinam Sankirtan, the congregational chanting of the holy names of God.

We journey to sacred places like Mayapur in West Bengal, the birthplace of Lord Chaitanya, to immerse ourselves in the ecstatic kirtans, enlightening discourses, and the vibrant devotional atmosphere that marks this grand festival. It's an unparalleled opportunity to experience the living tradition of Bhakti.

The October Pilgrimage: Honoring Lord Krishna's Pastimes

Our October pilgrimage is a journey into the heart of "Vraja Mandal," the land of Lord Krishna's divine pastimes. This tour often allows us to participate in or experience the devotional fervor surrounding Sri Krishna Janmashtami, the appearance day of Lord Krishna, the Supreme Personality of Godhead. While Janmashtami typically falls in August/September, our October timing allows us to visit holy places like Vrindavan and Mathura during a spiritually potent period, often encompassing the sacred month of Kartika, known for its deep spiritual significance.

We visit temples, holy sites, and engage in practices that connect us to Lord Krishna's timeless lilas (pastimes), fostering a deeper personal connection and understanding of His divine nature and teachings.
